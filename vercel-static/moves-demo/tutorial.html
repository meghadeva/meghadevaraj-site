🌊 Overview

float it first,
plan when it clicks

Moves is for the earliest part of social planning: when someone has an idea, nobody wants to over-coordinate yet, and the group just needs a simple way to see whether there is real interest.

01

Start casual

Float a possible plan before it becomes a calendar invite, group text spiral, or logistics project.

02

Pick the circle

Share it with the people who fit the idea, whether that is close friends, a hobby group, or a weekend crew.

03

Read the room

Friends can quickly signal interest, uncertainty, or a pass, so the group sees momentum without pressure.

04

Make the move

When enough people are in, anyone can prompt the group to make it happen.

The point

Moves makes social planning feel lighter by separating “would people be into this?” from “let’s coordinate every detail right now.”
